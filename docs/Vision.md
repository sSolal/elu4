The grand vision for this game : 

This game is an entryway into 4D. It should be very progressive, completely immersive, and help you get acquainted with perception, navigation and manipulation in 4D.

While there will be a story in the game, I also want it to be, as much as possible, an sandbox for other people to play with 4D, create maps, level, and for people to play together. As such, the universe of the game should lend itself to being reused broadly.

Ideally, the game would be hosted on a server and validated levels, maps and props could be included in it.

The inspirations are Subnautica, Minecraft, but with elements borrowed from GTA, Asphalt, any plane game, and Outer Wilds


The game loop is Subnautica's : you have a homebase (a first one at the beginning, and you'll unlock others) and you go out, then come back really often, to get familiar with the landscape.

You first arrive in a forest, where a fairy welcomes you. Are you up yet ? You have to turn your head until you see it. Various exercises about looking at it from different angles : 
first "can you hear me ?" "Can you see me ?", and once you've seen her she says : okay ! keep looking at me, there are some things I want to show you ! (Backstory to invent)

She somehow makes you make a full turn in one direction, then in another. Then follow in multiple directions. Then look up to the sky, where you see the Citadel in which some future adventure will take place. 
Then to the ground. Then she tells you to follow her, and to not step aside because we're on a cliff. You go forward for a moment. Then you reach a point where she says "stop !" and you have to turn until there is some road in front
of you again (still on that edge of a mountain).

You have to do that for a while. Then you arrive on a plateau. She says she's hungry, and she wants you to go grab some fruits. There are trees everywhere, and always a slight indicator of where the fairy is. You grab some fruits and bring them back, she becomes your friend and lands on you shoulder, then guide you through the forest with directions (left, right, reach that tree, etc...).

Then, you finally arrive in a home. That's the first time you're in an interior, and you look at every object (you learn strafing there).
